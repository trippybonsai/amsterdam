Why: Amsterdam's most acclaimed Indonesian. The rijsttafel is 15–20 small dishes built around rice. Signature dishes: daging rendang (slow-cooked Sumatran beef), ayam madu (honey chicken), sate kambing (goat satay), gado-gado, sambal goreng kentang. Bintang beer. Pandan panna cotta for dessert.

06 Dietary notes

Onion intolerance (one of you): alliums — onion, shallot, garlic, leek, spring onion — are foundational in Indonesian and most Dutch cooking. Always say at booking AND again at the table: "Allergy to onion, shallot, garlic — all alliums. Please flag any dishes that contain them." Dutch staff are generally great with allergies.

For Blauw (Saturday rijsttafel): the rijsttafel will be difficult — most dishes contain alliums, including the garnish bawang goreng (fried shallots) sprinkled on top. Call ahead. Options: order à la carte instead, or ask them to build a custom plate.

Drinks (no caffeine, no alcohol): hot chocolate is excellent in Amsterdam — proper warm milk with melted chocolate flakes ("warme chocomel"). Fresh OJ widely available — look for "verse jus d'orange". Alcohol-free beer: try Vandestreek 0.0% or Brouwerij 't IJ's alcohol-free range.

Drinks (coffee + beer): Amsterdam is a serious coffee city. Scandinavian Embassy, Lot Sixty One, Toki, Back to Black are excellent. Local craft: Brouwerij 't IJ, Oedipus, Two Chefs.

07 Coffeeshops

Quick rules: Bring photo ID, every coffeeshop checks. Tourists still allowed in Amsterdam. Indoor tobacco is illegal — most sell pure pre-rolled joints, edibles, or expect you to vape (bringing your own vape is fine). Consumption on premises is the norm. Edibles hit harder and slower — don't double-dose.
